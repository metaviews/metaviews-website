[Bitcoin: Bubble or Bank?](http://www.cbc.ca/thecurrent/episode/2013/04/11/bitcoin-bubble-or-bank/)Call it a currency without a country.</p><p>Bitcoins … digital tokens that are traded over the Internet … can’t be stuffed in your wallet, or crammed in your pocket.</p><p>But that hasn’t stopped the virtual coinage from sending ripples through the financial world.</p><p>Early this year you could buy a bitcoin online for $15.</p><p>But its value has surged — spiking upwards of $250 this week.</p><p>With more and more people looking to shelter themselves from a jittery global economy an increasing number of these virtual bucks are finding real-world homes.</p><p>Just ask Alex Likhtenstein.</p><p>He co-owns EVR Bar in Manhattan, and allows people to pay their tabs in bitcoin.</p><p>For more on this, we were joined by Jesse Hirsh.</p><p>He is a technology correspondent for CBC.He’s also co-founder of Academy of the Impossible, a peer-to-peer learning facility in Toronto.
