Not just the business model‚Äîthough that‚Äôs a disaster‚Äîbut the very structure of what we call <em>news</em>. The way it's gathered, framed, and delivered. The incentives that shape it. The audiences it courts. The reality it constructs.</p><p>And in that wreckage, authoritarianism thrives.</p><p>Trumpian fascism didn't just rise <em>in spite</em> of the news industry‚Äîit rose <em>through</em> it. Fueled by outrage cycles. Framed by false equivalence. Enabled by pundits chasing clicks. The spectacle overwhelmed the signal. But fascism isn‚Äôt an event. It‚Äôs a pattern. So is systemic collapse. So is climate disruption. So is the slow normalization of cruelty.</p><p>A neurodivergent newsroom would be built to detect these. Not just report what happened, but <em>why it happened, how it‚Äôs connected, and where it‚Äôs going</em>. Customizable interfaces, multiple modes of input and output, nonlinear narratives, and neurodiverse pathways through information.</p><p>Imagine a story you could <em>read, watch, feel,</em> or <em>map</em>. A story that meets you where you are, whether you‚Äôre overstimulated or deep in focus mode. Accessibility isn‚Äôt a feature. It‚Äôs a politics.</p><h3><strong>Lived Truth, Not Manufactured Objectivity</strong></h3><p>The myth of neutrality has always served power. This news model would do away with it entirely. Instead, it would embrace positionality, voice, and lived experience‚Äînot as bias, but as <em>context</em>.</p><p>Stories would come with metadata: who‚Äôs telling it, from where, through what lens. They gain new context. They resonate differently as time passes. This news service would be structured like an open-source project: versioned, forked, revisited, extended.</p><p>You could trace the evolution of a narrative over months or years. Truth isn‚Äôt static. It‚Äôs something we steward, together.</p><h3><strong>Community, Not Spectatorship</strong></h3><p>Instead of broadcasting to a passive audience, this news service would activate <em>co-creators</em>. Readers could annotate, expand, or remix stories. They could propose new patterns to explore. Research and storytelling would be <em>collaborative</em>, not extractive.</p><p>It wouldn‚Äôt ask: <em>what will get clicks?</em><br>It would ask: <em>what do we need to understand together?</em></p><h3><strong>Resisting Fascism by Refusing Its Logic</strong></h3><p>Trumpian fascism thrives on binaries: true/false, us/them, chaos/order. The neurodivergent mind often sees <em>multiplicity</em> instead‚Äîgray areas, nonlinear causality, subtle shifts in mood and meaning.</p><p>This kind of news would defy fascism not by debating it on its terms, but by offering a different grammar altogether. A commons. A publishing model. A political force.</p><p>The neurodivergent news service could be it.</p><p>Not as an accommodation, but as an <em>advance</em>.<br>Not as a niche, but as a <em>revolution</em>.<br>Not as a reaction, but as a reimagination.</p><div id="youtube2-lIOb2zIkYpI" class="youtube-wrap" data-attrs="{&quot;videoId&quot;:&quot;lIOb2zIkYpI&quot;,&quot;startTime&quot;:null,&quot;endTime&quot;:null}" data-component-name="Youtube2ToDOM"><div class="youtube-inner"><iframe src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/lIOb2zIkYpI?rel=0&amp;autoplay=0&amp;showinfo=0&amp;enablejsapi=0" frameborder="0" loading="lazy" gesture="media" allow="autoplay; fullscreen" allowautoplay="true" allowfullscreen="true" width="728" height="409"></iframe></div></div><h2><strong>The Neurodivergent News Manifesto</strong></h2><p><em>Because the future won‚Äôt be reported the way the past was.</em></p><p>We live in a world where traditional news amplifies power, anesthetizes resistance, and recycles lies as headlines.
