Invocation</strong></h3><p>Two hundred issues deep.<br>Two hundred gestures into the void, or the chorus.<br>Two hundred attempts to name what cannot be priced.</p><p>We mark this moment not to measure success—<br>but to resist the very urge to measure.</p><p>We have been taught that worth comes back to us in numbers.<br>In growth. In gain. In Return on Investment.</p><p>But what if it doesn’t come back?<br>What if it never needed to?</p><p>What if value is not something that returns—<br>but something that remains?</p><h3><strong>II. Renunciation</strong></h3><p>Let it be said plainly:<br><strong>ROI, as we have known it, is dead.</strong></p><p>It died of overuse.<br>Of turning every act of care into a transaction.<br>Every gift into a gamble.<br>Every idea into an asset.</p><p>ROI taught us to withhold our best<br>until we were promised something in return.<br>It made us calculate connection.<br>To monetize meaning.<br>To optimize everything—including ourselves.</p><p>It offered the illusion of control,<br>but demanded we live only for what could be measured.</p><p>Let this be a ritual of release.</p><blockquote><p>We renounce the tyranny of metrics.<br>We renounce the economy as god.<br>We renounce the lie that growth is purpose.</p></blockquote><p>Burn the spreadsheets.<br>Silence the dashboards.<br>Compost the KPI.</p><h3><strong>III. Reclamation</strong></h3><p>The acronym is not the enemy.<br>Only its owners.</p><p>We reclaim ROI—not as Return, but as Reorientation.</p><p>Let ROI now stand for:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Rituals of Interdependence</strong><br>We invest in each other without demand for repayment.<br>We exchange not in currency, but in care.<br>Value flows—not in circles of capital, but in webs of reciprocity.</p></li><li><p><strong>Refusal of Optimization</strong><br>We do not smooth our edges to suit the algorithm.<br>We do not trim complexity for convenience.<br>We embrace the inefficient, the slow, the relational.</p></li><li><p><strong>Reclamation of Intimacy</strong><br>We dare to get close.<br>To linger.<br>To listen.<br>To be changed in ways that no metric can capture.</p></li></ul><p>This new ROI cannot be charted.<br>It can only be lived.</p><h3><strong>IV. Affirmation</strong></h3><p>If this sounds like a new direction,<br>it is not.<br>It is the road we’ve been walking all along.</p><p>Quietly.<br>Together.<br>Sometimes unknowingly.</p><p>This isn’t a pivot.<br>It’s a spell breaking.<br>A system unraveling.<br>A future remembering itself.</p><p>Return on Investment was a useful lie—<br>for those who benefit from other people’s labor, land, and love.</p><p>But the truth was always older:<br>Value lives in relationship.<br>In repair.<br>In refusal.</p><h3><strong>V. Vow</strong></h3><p>We vow not to measure what matters,<br>but to make what matters measurable only through living it.</p><p>We vow to grow without scaling,<br>to connect without converting,<br>to give without expecting.</p><p>We vow to make meaning outside the market.</p><p>Not because it’s profitable.<br>But because it’s possible.</p><p>Because it’s necessary.</p><p>Because there are forms of value that will never return—<br>but always remain.</p><div id="tiktok-iframe?media=1&amp;app=1&amp;url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.tiktok.com%2F%40total_hypocrisy%2Fvideo%2F7514890379909451038&amp;key=e27c740634285c9ddc20db64f73358dd" class="tiktok-wrap outer" data-attrs="{&quot;url&quot;:&quot;https://www.tiktok.com/@total_hypocrisy/video/7514890379909451038&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;6/11/25.
