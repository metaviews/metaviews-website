<section name="30c6" class="section section--body section--first section--last"><div class="section-divider"><hr class="section-divider"></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn"><figure name="9301" id="9301" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--h4"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*lSbrrDVb00FDHE2L2OGm7Q.png" data-width="1024" data-height="1024" data-is-featured="true" src="/assets/medium/2024-01-30-navigating-a-future-of-climate-catastrophe-and-fascism/1-lSbrrDVb00FDHE2L2OGm7Q-92042907.png"><figcaption class="imageCaption">Climate anxiety is real</figcaption></figure><p name="c9d8" id="c9d8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Many of us feel a growing anxiety about the future, particularly in light of the escalating climate crisis and the rise of extreme right-wing politics. Yet we cannot succumb to the paralysis that comes from analysis, instead we should focus our analysis on change, and a path towards a better future.</p><h4 name="7667" id="7667" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p">Understanding Climate Change Anxiety through Deviation Amplification and Cognitive Dissonance</h4><p name="e25d" id="e25d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Deviation Amplification</strong>: Small environmental neglects, like excessive use of fossil fuels, amplify over time, leading to significant climate changes.</p><p name="dd50" id="dd50" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Cognitive Dissonance</strong>: When confronted with the realities of climate change, individuals may experience a conflict between their actions (like using non-renewable resources) and the knowledge of environmental impact, leading to cognitive dissonance.</p><h4 name="eb20" id="eb20" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p">Dealing with Right-Wing Extremism:</h4><p name="9a6c" id="9a6c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Deviation Amplification in Politics</strong>: Small shifts in political discourse can gradually lead to the normalization of extreme views.</p><p name="9c55" id="9c55" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Cognitive Dissonance in Political Beliefs</strong>: People may struggle when their long-held beliefs clash with the emerging political realities, such as witnessing the rise of extreme ideologies that contradict democratic principles.</p><h4 name="bc32" id="bc32" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p">Transformative Learning as a Path to Change:</h4><p name="a6db" id="a6db" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4">Transformative learning goes beyond merely accumulating facts. It’s about a fundamental change in how we see and interact with the world. Regarding climate change, this might mean shifting from a mindset of consumption to one of conservation, realizing the impact of individual actions on the global ecosystem. In politics, it involves critically examining one’s beliefs and understanding the roots and impacts of extremist ideologies, fostering a culture of empathy and open-mindedness.</p><h4 name="4635" id="4635" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p">Expanding Social Capital for Collective Action:</h4><p name="637e" id="637e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4">Social capital is the glue that holds communities together in pursuit of common goals. In tackling climate change, communities with strong social bonds can more effectively organize local conservation efforts, share sustainable practices, and support each other in lifestyle changes.</p><p name="01bc" id="01bc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">In resisting right-wing extremism, social capital helps in creating inclusive spaces where diverse viewpoints are respected and dialogues are fostered, reducing the isolation and alienation that often fuel extremist views.</p><p name="ae2f" id="ae2f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">By deepening our understanding through transformative learning and strengthening our communities’ social capital, we can more effectively address the challenges of climate change and political extremism, leading to a more hopeful and proactive stance towards the future.</p></div></div></section>
